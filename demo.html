Good vs. Bad Designs

Below are some good and bad designs based on our findings.

Readability

Short Chunks Of Text Are Easier To Read

Information presented in short paragraphs smaller columns are easier to process.

Break information about different topics into different chunks.

Avoid long blocks of text

Long blocks of text can get super hard to read, especially if multiple topics are combined into one long paragraph. Default backgrounds (in muted colors)

Bright and Distracting Backgrounds

Bright red background with obnoxious color vs muted background. This is also an accessibility concern for those who can't distinguish between similar colors. This also includes using a colorful distracting background image or moving gif/video.

Muted and Readable Backgrounds

This is much easier to read. There are no longer random colors drawing your attention away from the website's content.

Finding Information

Complex dropdown menus

A dropdown menu with too many options or certain options hidden away is very difficult to use. It could lead your user astray and either prevent them from finding the information they need or cause them to get distracted.

Search bars

Of the individuals we interviewed, all of them preferred to use search bars. If your content is searchable, providing a search bar helps users get to the information they want more efficiently and directly.

Column Layout

Sidebars and Multi-Column Layouts

Multiple columns, especially with distracting images or links to other pages, can be very distracting and cause your user to click away.

Instead, only use sidebars or multiple column if you're comparing information or your sidebar serves a purpose apart from the main content.

One Column Layouts

Having your website layout be one column makes it easier to read and less distracting.

Other Distracting Elements

Popups and Notifications

Popups and notifications can easily distract someone with ADHD. Avoid unnecessary elements that link to other pages.

Relevant Links

Provide relevant links to the content you're presenting. Don't let your user forget about your content while they're browsing some other topic!
